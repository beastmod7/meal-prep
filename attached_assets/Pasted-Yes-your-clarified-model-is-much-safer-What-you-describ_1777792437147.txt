Yes — **your clarified model is much safer**.

What you described is basically:

> **Advance payment for a fixed service package → consumption of service → cancellation → refund of unused service value to original payment method.**

That is **not the same thing as a wallet**.

## My view

This should **generally not be treated as an RBI PPI wallet** if you keep it exactly like this:

* User buys **20 meals / 30 meals**, not ₹3,000 wallet balance.
* User cannot add money.
* User cannot transfer balance.
* User cannot withdraw balance.
* User cannot use leftover value on random merchants like a payment instrument.
* Refund happens only after **plan cancellation**.
* Refund goes only to the **original payment method**.
* The app shows **meals remaining**, not “cash balance.”

That is a normal prepaid service/subscription model.

RBI’s PPI FAQ says closed-system instruments used only to buy goods/services from the issuing entity, with no cash withdrawal and no third-party settlement, are not classified as payment systems requiring RBI authorisation. The old RBI circular language similarly says closed-system instruments are for goods/services from the issuer and do not permit cash withdrawal/redemption or third-party settlement. ([Reserve Bank of India][1])

## The one thing to be careful about

The word **“refund”** is okay.

The word **“withdraw”** is not.

So don’t say:

> Withdraw your remaining balance.

Say:

> Cancel plan and get refund for unused meals as per policy.

That keeps the legal character as **service cancellation**, not money redemption.

## Best UI structure

Show this:

```text
30 Meal Plan

Meals used: 3
Meals remaining: 27

Cancel plan
Refund estimate: ₹2,430
Refund will be sent to your original payment method.
```

Avoid this:

```text
Wallet balance: ₹2,430
Withdraw balance
Use balance elsewhere
Transfer balance
```

The first one is boring and safe. The second one is fintech cosplay. RBI does not enjoy cosplay.

## Terms should clearly say

Add something like this in your terms:

> The meal package is an advance purchase of meal services. Unused meals may be eligible for refund upon cancellation according to the applicable refund policy. The package is not a wallet, prepaid payment instrument, stored-value account, cash balance, or transferable payment method. Refunds, where applicable, will be processed only to the original payment method.

## My blunt answer

Your model is **probably fine from a PPI-wallet angle** as long as you keep it as:

> **Meal package → meal entitlement → cancellation refund**

Not:

> **Money stored → money spent → money withdrawn**

The bigger issues for you will likely be **consumer protection, refund policy clarity, GST/accounting, restaurant settlement, and food-service liability**, not RBI wallet regulation.

Still, before launch, get a fintech/payment lawyer to review the terms and actual money flow. But product-wise, this is the clean direction.
