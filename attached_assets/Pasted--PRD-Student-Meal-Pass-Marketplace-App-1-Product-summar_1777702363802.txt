# PRD: Student Meal Pass Marketplace App

## 1. Product summary

**Product concept:**
A mobile-first meal pass app for students where they can buy prepaid meal passes, schedule meals from partner restaurants, cancel upcoming meals one day before for free, and get unused meal value carried forward or refunded.

**Core promise:**

> Pay once. Eat flexibly. Cancel tomorrow’s meal by tonight. Your unused meals stay yours.

This should **not** feel like Zomato/Swiggy ordering every day. It should feel like a **student food operating system**.

## 2. Product positioning

### Current market problem

Students have three bad options:

1. Order every day on food delivery apps and pay per order.
2. Eat hostel/mess food even when they hate it.
3. Negotiate manually with restaurants/tiffin providers over WhatsApp.

The missing product is:

> A flexible monthly meal system where students can subscribe to food access without being punished for skipped meals.

### Product category

Not exactly food delivery. Not exactly wallet. Not exactly subscription.

Best category:

```txt
Student Meal Pass Platform
```

Internally:

```txt
Meal pass + scheduled ordering + restaurant settlement ledger
```

## 3. Important payment assumption

For iOS/Android, you should use **Razorpay / PhonePe / PayU / similar payment gateway**, not RevenueCat. Apple’s guideline says apps selling physical goods or services consumed outside the app must use payment methods other than in-app purchase, such as Apple Pay or credit card entry. Google Play says Play Billing is for digital goods, and specifically lists physical goods and food delivery as not supported by Google Play Billing. ([Apple Developer][1])

For India, be careful with the “stored balance” design. RBI says non-bank PPI issuers generally require authorization, while closed-system instruments used only to buy goods/services from the issuing entity and without cash withdrawal are not classified as a regulated payment system. Your design should be reviewed by a payments lawyer/CA before launch, because “meal balance across restaurants” can accidentally start smelling like a wallet. ([Reserve Bank of India][2])

## 4. Product goal

Build a trusted meal pass system where:

```txt
Student buys meal pass
↓
Student schedules meals
↓
Student cancels tomorrow’s meal before cutoff for free
↓
Late cancellations incur fee
↓
Restaurant gets predictable demand
↓
Platform settles restaurants weekly
↓
Unused paid meal value carries forward or is refundable
```

## 5. MVP decision

Do **not** launch as:

```txt
Add ₹6,000 to wallet
```

Launch as:

```txt
Buy a Meal Pass
```

Because “wallet” creates regulatory, trust, and accounting complexity.

Better user-facing language:

```txt
30 Meal Pass
₹6,000
Valid for 45 days
Cancel next day’s meal by 10 PM tonight for free
Unused paid meals carry forward or can be refunded
```

## 6. Core product rules

### 6.1 Meal pass unit

The pass should be based on **meal credits**, not days.

Bad:

```txt
₹6,000 monthly plan for 30 days
```

Good:

```txt
30 Meal Pass for ₹6,000
Valid for 45 days
```

Why? Because students don’t consume “days.” They consume meals.

### 6.2 Meal credit value

Example:

```txt
Pass price: ₹6,000
Meals included: 30
Effective meal value: ₹200 per meal
```

If the user eats 18 meals:

```txt
Consumed: 18 × ₹200 = ₹3,600
Unused: 12 meals = ₹2,400 remaining value
```

### 6.3 Premium meals

Some meals may cost more than the standard credit value.

Example:

```txt
Standard meal credit value: ₹200
Premium thali: ₹240
User pays: 1 meal credit + ₹40 extra
```

Or:

```txt
Premium thali uses 1.2 credits
```

For MVP, use **credit + extra rupee payment**. Easier to understand.

## 7. Cancellation policy

This is the heart of the product.

### Standard rule

```txt
Cancel by 10 PM the previous day: Free
Cancel after 10 PM previous day: Late cancellation fee
No-show / restaurant already prepared: Full deduction
```

### Lunch example

Meal: Tuesday lunch

```txt
Free cancellation: Until Monday 10 PM
Late cancellation: Monday 10 PM to Tuesday 9 AM
Full charge/no-show: After Tuesday 9 AM
```

### Dinner example

Meal: Tuesday dinner

```txt
Free cancellation: Until Monday 10 PM
Late cancellation: Monday 10 PM to Tuesday 3 PM
Full charge/no-show: After Tuesday 3 PM
```

### MVP simplified rule

Use one clean rule first:

```txt
Cancel by 10 PM the previous night: Free
After that: 50% meal credit deducted
After prep/dispatched/no-show: 100% meal credit deducted
```

This is simple enough for students and protective enough for restaurants. Not NASA tiffin ops.

## 8. Refund policy

Refunds should be based on **unused paid meal credits**, not skipped days.

### Refund formula

```txt
Refundable amount =
Unused paid meal credits × effective paid value per credit
- late cancellation fees
- non-refundable payment/platform fees, if any
```

### Example

```txt
30 Meal Pass = ₹6,000
1 meal credit = ₹200

Meals eaten: 18
Late cancellations: 2 at 50% each = ₹200 total
Free cancellations: 5 = ₹0 deducted

Total consumed/charged:
18 meals = ₹3,600
Late cancellation fees = ₹200

Refundable amount:
₹6,000 - ₹3,600 - ₹200 = ₹2,200
```

### Refund UX copy

```txt
Refund Summary

Pass purchased: ₹6,000
Meals included: 30
Meals used: 18
Late cancellation charges: ₹200
Unused paid value: ₹2,200

Refund goes back to your original payment method in 3–7 working days.
Bonus credits are non-refundable.
```

## 16. Ledger architecture

You need two different ledgers.

### 16.1 User meal ledger

Purpose: user balance and trust.

Events:

```txt
pass_purchase
meal_scheduled
meal_locked
meal_debited
free_cancellation_credit_restored
late_cancellation_debit
full_no_show_debit
restaurant_cancel_credit_restored
refund_requested
refund_processed
bonus_credit_added
bonus_credit_expired
```

### 16.2 Business accounting ledger

Purpose: money truth.

Events:

```txt
cash_collected
customer_liability_created
meal_revenue_recognized
restaurant_payable_created
platform_commission_earned
refund_liability_created
refund_processed
gateway_fee_recorded
settlement_paid
```

### Golden rule

```txt
User payment is not revenue until meal delivery/charge event happens.
```

If user pays ₹6,000 and eats ₹3,000 worth:

```txt
Customer liability remaining: ₹3,000
Revenue recognized: only platform commission on consumed meals
Restaurant payable: restaurant share on consumed meals
```

## 18. Payment flow

### Pass purchase flow

```txt
User selects pass
↓
Backend creates payment order
↓
User pays via Razorpay/UPI/card
↓
Webhook confirms payment
↓
Backend creates meal_pass
↓
Backend creates user_ledger event: pass_purchase
↓
User sees active pass
```

### Meal debit flow

```txt
Meal scheduled
↓
Free cancellation window passes
↓
Meal locks
↓
Restaurant accepts/prepares
↓
Meal delivered/no-show/late cancel
↓
Credits deducted
↓
Restaurant payable created
↓
Platform commission recognized
```

### Refund flow

```txt
User requests refund
↓
System calculates refundable paid value
↓
Admin/system approves
↓
Gateway refund initiated
↓
Ledger records refund_processed
↓
Meal pass credits reduced
```

## 22. Key edge cases

### Payment successful but webhook delayed

Show:

```txt
Payment received. Activating your pass...
```

Backend should reconcile gateway status.

### User cancels one minute before cutoff

Allow it. Don’t be shady.

### User tries to cancel after cutoff

Show fee clearly before action.

### Restaurant cancels after accepting

Restore full credit. Optionally give goodwill credit.

### User no-shows repeatedly

Add risk flag.

Possible policy:

```txt
After 3 no-shows in 30 days, user must confirm meals manually.
```

### Restaurant quality complaints spike

Auto-pause restaurant from new scheduling until admin review.

### User requests refund after bonus used

Refund only unused paid value, not bonus.

### User schedules beyond pass validity

Allow only if meal date is within validity, or prompt renewal.

## 27. Final product rulebook

Use this as the operating constitution:

```txt
1. Users buy meal credits, not calendar days.
2. Free cancellation is allowed until 10 PM the previous day.
3. Late cancellation has a visible fee.
4. No-show or prep-start cancellation can consume full credit.
5. Restaurant cancellation restores user credit fully.
6. Unused paid meal value carries forward or is refundable.
7. Bonus credits are non-refundable.
8. Refunds go only to original payment method.
9. User top-up/pass money is liability until meals are consumed.
10. Restaurant settlement happens only for delivered/chargeable meals.
```

## 28. Final recommendation

Build the MVP as:

```txt
Meal Pass + Previous-Day Cancellation + Carry-Forward + Refundable Paid Value + Restaurant Settlement Ledger
```

Do **not** build:

```txt
Open wallet
Unlimited monthly food subscription
Daily food ordering clone
```

The killer UX is this:

```txt
You have 12 meals left.
Cancel tomorrow’s meal by 10 PM tonight for free.
Unused paid meals stay yours.
```

That’s the trust wedge. That’s the product.
