Beast, for this kind of monthly meal-package app, your restaurant partner UI should be built like a **mini GST + settlement command center**, not just “orders and payouts.” If you build it clean from day one, your CA will love you and your future audit will not turn into a horror movie.

Important: I’m not your CA/lawyer, but this is the product structure I’d design before getting it reviewed.

## First: decide your GST role clearly

For food delivery through an e-commerce operator, India has a specific rule: **restaurant services supplied through an e-commerce operator are covered under Section 9(5), where the e-commerce operator is liable to pay GST on restaurant services supplied through it**. CBIC Circular No. 167/23/2021 clarified that ECOs are liable to pay GST on restaurant services supplied through them, including supplies by unregistered restaurants. It also clarified that ECOs are not required to collect TCS under Section 52 for those restaurant services where the ECO pays tax under Section 9(5). ([CBIC GST][1])

So your accounting model should separate:

1. **Meal service value** — likely GST liability on platform/ECO side under 9(5), depending on exact model.
2. **Your commission/platform fee charged to restaurant** — your own supply/service to restaurant, usually separately invoiced with GST.
3. **Refunds/cancellations** — credit-note/reversal logic.
4. **Restaurant settlement** — net payable after commission, refunds, penalties, deductions, etc.

That separation is the whole game.

# What the restaurant partner UI should contain

## 1. Partner KYC + GST profile section

Each restaurant profile should have:

```text
Legal business name
Trade name
GSTIN
PAN
FSSAI license number
Registered address
Place of supply/state
Bank account details
UPI ID, optional
Restaurant branch ID
Whether GST registered: Yes/No
Composition taxpayer status, if applicable
Restaurant type: normal restaurant / cloud kitchen / specified premises / other
```

Why this matters: CBIC clarified that ECO liability can apply even when restaurant suppliers are unregistered, but you still need correct partner classification for reporting, settlement, invoices, and audit trail. ([CBIC GST][1])

Add a warning if GSTIN is missing:

> “GSTIN not provided. Tax treatment may differ. Contact support before activating payouts.”

Not fancy. Just clean.

## 4. Refund and cancellation module

Since your model allows refund after 2–3 meals, build a clear refund flow.

Restaurant should see:

```text

Refundable value
Cancellation fee, if any
Discount adjustment, if any
GST adjustment
Final refund amount
Refund destination: original payment method
Refund status

```

Use wording like:

> “Refund for unused meals after plan cancellation.”

Avoid:

> “Wallet withdrawal”
> “Balance withdrawal”
> “Cashout”

For GST alignment, refund events should generate credit-note style records where applicable. GSTN has also been improving invoice/credit-note handling through its Invoice Management System, so your internal records should treat cancellations and credit notes as first-class objects, not random support refunds. ([The Economic Times][2])

Restaurant UI is only half. Your company needs an admin compliance panel.

## Admin tax configuration

```text
GST treatment per service type
Restaurant service GST rate
Platform fee GST rate
Refund GST reversal rules
Credit note numbering
Invoice numbering
State-wise tax split: CGST/SGST/IGST
Section 9(5) flag

```

CBIC guidance clarifies that for restaurant services notified under Section 9(5), ECOs are not required to collect TCS under Section 52 for those supplies, but TCS can still matter for other goods/services not covered under 9(5). ([National Academy of Customs][3])

# The clean accounting flow

For a 30-meal package:

```text
1. Customer buys 30-meal package.
2. System creates package purchase + invoice record.
3. Each consumed meal creates redemption record.
4. Restaurant earns settlement only for eligible consumed/locked meals, depending on your contract.
5. If user cancels, system calculates unused meal refund.
6. Refund goes to original payment method.
7. Credit note/refund adjustment is generated.
8. Restaurant settlement is reduced if needed.
9. Monthly GST/accounting exports are generated.
```

This is sane.

---

# Final blunt recommendation

Build the restaurant portal around **three ledgers**:

1. **Meal ledger** — what was bought, consumed, refunded.
2. **Tax ledger** — invoice, GST, credit notes, tax treatment.
3. **Settlement ledger** — what restaurant earned, what you deducted, what you paid.

Do not just build “orders dashboard.” That’s amateur hour. For this business, accounting is the product’s skeleton. Without it, the whole thing walks weird.
